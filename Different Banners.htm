Oh this is a banger, bought a couple hangers so I can stare at my banner,

Majestic perfection wrapped around an impenetrable armor of imperfections,

Like I say I’m ancient and ain’t shit at a glance,

But if you take a look at what lies beneath you’ll find a nuanced core we call humanity.

Confusing isn’t it?

How we live for the moment but get bottled up in minutes taken out of context,

And God forbid you try to tackle the complexities without forewarning,

Are you with me or my enemy?

Are you a bro, a sis, do I gotta get the tool just in case but before that let me take classes so I don’t miss the chance to fix.

Are we on the same level, do we see eye to eye or do I have to get my guys to assist you with your… education

From the looks of it you need rearranging, listening to the opposition got your mind racing and changing its melody, its tune, its proper thought process, but don’t worry, I can fix that.

Piles of books written by yours truly, full of our true history.

And, remember, fuck the naysayers, they’re just haters, they aint us.

What do they know?

Their stories are full of lies and misdirections, least to mention blaspheming on the daily, but real quick yo.

I’ll die for this shit and so should you, lone band of brothers and sisters on top of a hill facing an eternal Sun armed with guns that never run out of ammunition, cause there’s no safety, just automatic fire and the roars of the righteous blotting out the sound that comes below…

You hear crying?

False advertisements from the enemy so don’t heed what they say. Keep your eyes trained down the sights and that trigger finger curled tight.

Don’t let up son, we got this in the bag, mowing them like grass, their ashes will be fertilizer for the new garden of our growing empire.

 

 

This another banger from another angle.

Tired from the climb up we scrape hands against unforgiving rocks and the blood pools blow us.

It’s just one big… stain on our history.

Still, we lock eyes with our enemies and shoulder on, firing bursts from closed eyes and tear marked faces, cheeks pinched and with no replacements.

We’re the last and the banner I carry will stand,

Atop a hill where we don’t belong, lording over bodies of those that couldn’t get along.

Crying out to God asking why were we made to do this, but then, the sound of heretical guns break through.

Was this a test from the Most high? Putting my faith against the blasphemers that laugh as they slay His children gleefully.

I can see it in their eyes, and I ask forgiveness for wavering.

“Lord on this day, grant me the strength to carry out your mercy, and your word to the unfaithful that lay before me, let these blessed bullets find their mark, and remove sin from this plane.

For through your mercy can we grant those that rebel against your name the chance to repent.”

The prayer lifts the spirit and it spreads like wildfire, truth that His Word is true, so I don’t falter, I don’t waiver when we reach the top.

Screaming out encouragement through scripture, I let the Lord and his angels guide me as I send these wayward souls up.
